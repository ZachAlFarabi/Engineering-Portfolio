● Objectives

As a component of my honours project, flight-by-flight modelling of nz-loads were required for the Cessna-172. Knowledge and modelling concepts surrounding Stochastic models (including stochastic differential equations), random processes, monte-carlo simulations, and inverse transformation methods were used. Its usage, limitations, and parameters were discussed within documentation.

● Technical Details

  • Key computation analyst. Deriving a process of transforming a nominal FAA DOT dataset to a flight-by-flight spectrum. This includes statistical derivation, and inverse transformation over monte-carlo simulations.
  • Utilised and learned ensemble averaging over differet monte-carlo spectra to create an averaged specrtum. Using white noise to create variance within the simulation to simulate uncertainty.
  • Learned modelling techniques using the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck model and jump diffusion model, and their properties for contextual usage.
  • Mathematically derived a custom SDE model using a combination of the OU-model and jump diffusion model, acting as turbulence loads, which have controllable parameters over stability steady states.
